# CLA Frequently Asked Questions

**D-PC Messenger Contributor License Agreement - FAQ**

---

## General Questions

### Q: Why does D-PC Messenger need a CLA?

**A:** To enable dual licensing (open source + commercial). Without your permission, we cannot offer commercial licenses that include your code. This supports sustainable open-source development (similar to Qt, MongoDB, GitLab).

### Q: Does this mean you'll close-source my code?

**A:** No! Your code will **always** be available under GPL/LGPL/AGPL. The commercial license is an **additional** option for enterprise customers who need it (e.g., to embed in proprietary products).

### Q: Can I still use my code elsewhere?

**A:** Yes! You retain copyright and can use your code in any other project, under any license you choose.

### Q: What's the difference between CLA and DCO?

| Aspect | CLA (D-PC uses) | DCO (Linux uses) |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Signing** | One-time agreement | Per-commit sign-off |
| **Dual licensing** | ✅ Enabled | ❌ Not enabled |
| **Patent grant** | ✅ Included | ❌ Not included |
| **Corporate tracking** | ✅ Built-in | ❌ Manual |

## Process Questions

### Q: How do I sign the CLA?

**A:** Two options:
1. **Recommended:** Open a pull request, click "I agree" when CLA Assistant prompts you
2. **Manual:** Email signed agreement to legoogmiha@gmail.com

### Q: Do I need to sign for every contribution?

**A:** No. You only sign once. CLA Assistant remembers for all future contributions.

### Q: What about tiny contributions (typo fixes, docs)?

**A:** Minor contributions (typos, documentation edits) generally don't require a CLA. CLA Assistant will still prompt you, but you can proceed with "I agree" in seconds.

## Corporate Contributions

### Q: What if my employer owns my work?

**A:** You need one of:
1. Written permission from your employer (keep a copy), OR
2. Your employer signs a Corporate CLA

### Q: How does my company sign a Corporate CLA?

**A:** Email [legoogmiha@gmail.com](mailto:legoogmiha@gmail.com) with:
- Company name and legal entity
- Contact person (name, title, email)
- Expected number of contributors
- Brief description of contribution plans

## Legal Questions

### Q: Can I revoke the CLA later?

**A:** No. The license grants are irrevocable. However:
- Your code remains under open-source license forever
- You can stop contributing at any time
- Your copyright remains yours

### Q: What about contributions I made before the CLA existed?

**A:**
- **Major contributors:** We may ask you to sign retroactively
- **Minor contributions:** Covered under implied license
- **Uncertain cases:** We'll contact you individually

### Q: Do project maintainers sign the CLA?

**A:** Yes! Everyone who contributes code signs the CLA, including core maintainers and the founder.

### Q: What if I disagree with a commercial license sale?

**A:** The CLA gives the Project the right to make licensing decisions. However:
- Core software **always** remains open source (GPL/LGPL/AGPL)
- Commercial licenses enable sustainable development
- Community governance decides major licensing changes

**A:** Yes. Similar CLAs are used by:

| Project | CLA Type | Dual Licensing |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Qt** | CLA | ✅ Yes (LGPL + Commercial) |

| **MySQL** | CLA | ✅ Yes (GPL + Commercial) |
| **Apache Software Foundation** | CLA | ❌ No (pure open source) |

### Q: What about documentation contributions?

**A:** Documentation contributions generally don't require a CLA (they're under CC BY 4.0). However, if documentation includes executable code samples, the CLA may apply to those code portions.

## Technical Questions

### Q: How does CLA Assistant work?

**A:**
1. You open a pull request
2. CLA Assistant bot checks if you've signed
3. If not, it comments with a link to agree
4. You click "I agree" (GitHub OAuth)
5. Bot updates the PR status to "passed"
6. All future PRs are automatically approved

### Q: What if CLA Assistant is down?

**A:** Maintainers can manually override the check, or you can use the manual signing process (email).

## Privacy Questions

### Q: What information do you collect?

**A:** When you sign via CLA Assistant:
- GitHub username
- GitHub email (public)
- Agreement timestamp

When you sign manually:
- Full legal name
- Email
- Date

### Q: Is my CLA information public?

**A:** No. Signed CLAs are stored securely and not publicly disclosed. CLA Assistant stores data in accordance with GitHub's privacy policy.
